Nanobubbles

Nanobubbles are a novel type of nanoscale bubble system. They have a typical mean spherical diameter of 100-200 nanometers and they exist in bulk liquid. The most peculiar characteristic of these bulk nanobubbles is their extraordinary longevity. Whilst the lifetime of macrobubbles (>1 mm) is of the order of minutes and that of microbubbles (1-1000 microns) is on the order of seconds, nanobubbles have been reported to last for weeks and months. The Young-Laplace equation, however, predicts a huge inner gas pressure (e.g., around 30 atm, inside a typical 100 nm nanobubble in pure water) and, consequently, bubble dynamics theory of Epstein-Plesset predicts that they would dissolve extremely quickly on a timescale of about 1-100 Âµs. In addition, nanobubble possesses high surface to volume ratio (~6/d), high mass transfer coefficient, long lasting stability and negative surface charge. Owing to these peculiar properties of nanobubbles, it has a wide range of applications in the field of water treatment, agriculture, froth flotation, etc.

Long term stability

One of the peculiar properties of bulk nanobubbles is extra-longevity as reported in the literature. The plausible reason could due the surface negative charge which inhibits the bubble coarsening mechanism like Ostwald ripening, coalescence etc. There are several advantages of the stability of the bulk nanobubbles. For instance, it acts as a gas reservoir to maintain the water quality parameter constant for long time. Similarly, in flavor and fragrance. Furthermore, nanobubbles are found to be under random Brownian motion which transport the dissolved oxygen uniformly without any external moving parts.

Negative surface charge

Surface charge plays an important role in determining the colloidal stability of the bulk nanobubbles. Colloidal stability of a suspension depends on DLVO and non-DLVO forces namely van der Waals forces, electrostatic attractive forces, hydration forces, hydrophobic interaction and steric forces. The overall interaction potential for a suspension is usually denoted by algebraic sum of DLVO and non-DLVO interactions. Furthermore, the suspension stability depends on the interplay between these interactions. Electrical double layer can be altered by addition of salt and pH whereas addition of surfactant leads to stabilize the bubbles sterically and it is thus pH, salt and surfactant affects the zeta potential of the nanobubbles. Zeta potential of nanobubbles are seen to decrease on decreasing the value of pH. This is the usual behaviour for the colloidal suspension. Since the slipping plane is negatively charged at pH = 7 and therefore addition of H+ (by lowering pH values) neutralizes the negative charge. This, in turn, not only reduces the negative value of zeta potential but also weakens the electrostatic interaction potential between nanobubbles. On the other hand, zeta potential of nanobubbles in alkaline medium is observed to increase with the value of pH, i.e., -32 mV at p = 10. All in all, as expected the colloidal stability of nanobubbles relatively better than that of acidic medium. Stable nanobubbles are due to balance between electrostatic and Laplace pressure.

High surface area

The rate of gas transfer in the liquid depends on the interfacial area and therefore the size of the bubble. The interfacial area per unit volume for a spherical bubble is given by 6/d where d is the diameter of the bubble. Evidently, decreasing the size of bubble leads to increase in the surface area. For instance, if 10 micrometer bubble is reduced to 100 nm nanobubbles then the surface area would increase by 100 times. In other words, billions of nanobubbles can be accommodate in 10-micron bubble. The mass transfer coefficient which is a measure of gas transfer capacity is expected increase my 100-fold using nanobubbles.

As noted earlier, the size of the nanobubble play important role in mass transfer of gas to the liquid. High gas transfer efficacy

Owing to the smaller size of nanobubbles, the rate of mass transfer increases significantly and thus the overall gas transfer efficiency of the mass transfer. In other words, nanobubble offers less resistance to interfacial mass transfer and hence the gas transfer efficiency enhancement occurs. High oxidative capacity

The oxidative capacity is often measured in terms of the capacity of production of oxygen reactive spices (ROS). ROS are responsible for degradation of organic matters and kills the pathogen and bacteria. Depending upon the gas inside the bubble the ROS production can be manipulated for various applications. For instance, ozone nanobubble can be used for sterilization and flour cleaning in the hospitals. It is to be noted that nanobubbles technology doesn`t use any chemical to sterilize the water like many other methods which often use hydrogen peroxide, chlorine etc.
